# Disaster Response Framework

## Emergency Classification

### Level 1 - Minor Impact
- Local events (e.g., small fires)
- Neighborhood response sufficient
- 0-50 people affected

### Level 2 - Major Impact
- Citywide events (e.g., severe weather)
- Multi-agency response required
- 50-1000 people affected

### Level 3 - Critical Impact
- Regional/national events (e.g., earthquakes)
- Full emergency mobilization
- 1000+ people affected

## Response Phases

### Phase 1: Preparedness
- Develop emergency plans
- Stock supplies
- Train personnel
- Establish communication systems

### Phase 2: Response
- Alert and warning systems
- Evacuation procedures
- Emergency medical services
- Search and rescue operations

### Phase 3: Recovery
- Damage assessment
- Resource allocation
- Temporary housing
- Infrastructure repair

### Phase 4: Mitigation
- Risk assessment
- Building code updates
- Land use planning
- Community education

## Communication Protocols

Warning levels:
- Watch: Potential threat
- Warning: Imminent threat
- Emergency: Active danger

## Traffic Management During Disasters

Priority routes:
- Red: Emergency services only
- Yellow: Evacuation routes
- Green: Open for general use

Sensor placement:
- Major intersections
- Highway entrances
- Critical facilities
- Evacuation corridors

## Community Engagement

Citizens should:
- Know evacuation routes
- Have emergency kits ready
- Sign up for alerts
- Participate in drills

Follow official guidance during emergencies. Do not spread unverified information.
