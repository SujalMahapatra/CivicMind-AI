# WHO Air Quality Guidelines (2021 Update)

The World Health Organization (WHO) provides air quality guidelines to protect public health.

## Guideline Concentrations

### PM2.5 (Fine Particulate Matter)
- Annual mean: 5 μg/m³
- 24-hour average: 15 μg/m³

### PM10 (Coarse Particulate Matter)
- Annual mean: 15 μg/m³
- 24-hour average: 45 μg/m³

## Health Impacts

Exposure to air pollution causes:
- Respiratory diseases (asthma, bronchitis)
- Cardiovascular diseases
- Lung cancer
- Stroke

## At-Risk Populations

High-risk groups include:
- Children under 5 years
- Adults over 60 years
- People with pre-existing conditions
- Pregnant women

## Recommended Actions

When AQI exceeds 100:
1. Reduce outdoor activities
2. Use air purifiers indoors
3. Keep windows closed
4. Wear N95 masks when outside
